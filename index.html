🔓 The Phantom Hacker of Nepal: Kaju's Cyber Saga 💻

Published on: May 15, 2025

Kathmandu, Nepal — A mysterious figure has shaken the local digital scene. Known only by the alias "Kaju Hacker" 👤, this young tech enthusiast claims to have hacked into over 200+ social media accounts 🔓.

From Facebook and Instagram 📱 to email platforms, Kaju Hacker reportedly exploited weak passwords and overlooked security loopholes—all from a basic smartphone, not a high-end computer 💻.

"I learned everything from YouTube and Google," the hacker said anonymously. "This isn't about causing harm. It's about testing systems and seeing what's possible with just a phone." 🔍

Experts warn that while these actions are illegal ⚖️, they highlight urgent cybersecurity issues in Nepal. Despite having no formal IT education, Kaju Hacker's actions challenge organizations to rethink their digital defenses 🛡️.

Authorities have taken notice, but so far, no legal action has been announced. Whether he's a hero 🦸, a hacker 💀, or both — Kaju Hacker has certainly left his mark on Nepal's cyber landscape 🌐.
